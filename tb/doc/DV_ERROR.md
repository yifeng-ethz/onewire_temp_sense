# onewire_master_controller DV — Error / Reset / Fault Cases

**Companion docs:** [DV_PLAN.md](DV_PLAN.md), [DV_HARNESS.md](DV_HARNESS.md), [DV_BASIC.md](DV_BASIC.md), [DV_EDGE.md](DV_EDGE.md), [DV_PROF.md](DV_PROF.md), [DV_CROSS.md](DV_CROSS.md), [DV_REPORT.md](DV_REPORT.md), [BUG_HISTORY.md](BUG_HISTORY.md)
**Parent:** [DV_PLAN.md](DV_PLAN.md)
**ID Range:** `X001-X130`
**Total:** 130 cases (17 implemented / 0 waived)

This document covers the fault, reset, and illegal-access closure surface of the `onewire_master_controller` system: master CSR illegal access, TX/RX engine faults, init-pulse / presence faults, FIFO faults, ticket-lock starvation and forced unlock, controller state faults, CRC mismatch on the planned controller CRC hookup, controller-CSR illegal access, and Read-ROM probe failure modes. Cases trace directly to the `tx_engine`, `rx_engine`, `interface_hub`, `ticket_lock`, `pipe_handler`, and `processor`/`tx_flow`/`rx_flow` FSMs in the master and controller IPs.

**Methodology key:**
- **D** = Directed (hand-crafted fault injection, deterministic)
- **R** = Constrained-random (LCG-based PRNG; no SystemVerilog rand)

## 2. Master CSR Illegal Access (CSR-FAULT) -- 12 cases

Out-of-range descriptor fields (`tot_bytes > MAX_BUFFER_DEPTH`, `wire_id >= N_DQ_LINES`) and busy-guarded commits are silently suppressed by `proc_helper_range_checker` and the busy-guard branch. Bypass two-phase reads/writes, undefined-addr accesses, and PARACITIC_POWERING-vs-CSR conflicts are also exercised here. STATUS[8] is the canonical sticky for range-suppressed writes.
